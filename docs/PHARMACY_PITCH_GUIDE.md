# Pharmacy Pitch Guide

Use this as a short in-shop conversation guide. Keep it natural. Ask more than you talk.

## 1. How To Open

Start simple:

`Hello, I work with a local pharmacy system built to help with stock control, expiry tracking, and reliable daily sales operations. Are you using a system now, or are you still mixing software and manual records?`

Then listen.

## 2. What To Ask

Ask these questions:

- `What system are you using now?`
- `What frustrates you most about it?`
- `Do you have problems with expiry losses or stock mismatches?`
- `How do you handle backups today?`
- `If your system stops working, how badly does it affect business that day?`
- `How easy is it to train new staff on your current system?`

Your goal is to find pain, not to force a demo.

## 3. What To Say

Focus on these points:

- `This system is built for local pharmacy use, so your work does not depend on unstable internet.`
- `It helps you track stock by batch and expiry, so you can reduce avoidable losses.`
- `Sales, stock movements, and key changes are traceable, so staff accountability is better.`
- `It is designed to stay fast and practical for everyday pharmacy work.`
- `We can help set it up locally and support migration from your current records.`

Do not lead with “dashboard”, “AI”, or “modern UI”.

## 4. Why They Switch

Most owners move only when one or more of these is true:

- they are losing money from expiry
- stock records do not match physical stock
- their current system is slow
- staff make too many mistakes
- support from the current vendor is poor
- backups are weak or unclear

Speak to those problems directly.

## 5. Short Pitch

Use this:

`We help pharmacies run sales and inventory more reliably, especially stock tracking, expiry control, and daily accountability. The system runs locally, works for real shop operations, and we support setup and migration so switching is not chaotic.`

## 6. If They Say “We Already Have Software”

Say:

`That makes sense. Most pharmacies do. The real question is whether it is helping you control stock, expiry, and staff activity properly, or whether you are still losing money and time around it.`

## 7. If They Ask “Why Yours?”

Say:

`Because we focus on practical pharmacy operations: batch-aware stock, expiry visibility, reliable local setup, and easier accountability. We are not trying to sell you just another generic POS.`

## 8. If They Ask “Can You Move Our Data?”

Say:

`Yes, that is part of the process. We look at what you already have, clean what needs cleaning, and help move it in a controlled way so you do not start from scratch.`

## 9. How To Close

Do not push too hard. Close with:

`If you want, I can come back for a short demo focused on your own workflow and show you whether it actually solves your stock and expiry problems.`

## 10. What Not To Say

Avoid:

- `best pharmacy software`
- `AI-powered`
- `fully offline-ready` unless you explain it correctly
- `production-grade` or `perfect`
- too much technical talk
- attacking their current system too early

## 11. What You Are Really Selling

You are selling:

- less stock confusion
- less expiry loss
- more control
- easier daily work
- safer business operations
